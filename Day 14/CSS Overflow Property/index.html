The fog clung to London’s streets as Holmes and I hurried through the dim gaslight. "Observe, Watson," he murmured, pausing by a wrought-iron gate. "The footprints here are fresh, the stride uneven—a man in haste, burdened by guilt. And there, the faint glimmer of a dropped cufflink. Our quarry is near, and the mystery unravels." His keen eyes gleamed with triumph as we pressed onward.
